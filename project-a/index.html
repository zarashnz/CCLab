JELIFLORA

Scientific Name:

Jeliflora Lumina

Discovered by / at:

Dr. Araz Zanhahs, year 1801 near the Malayan Peninsula.

Family:

This is part of the story telling of your project. Add the relevant information for the creature you discovered.

Habitat:

It resides in a unique region of the ocean where sunlight reaches even the deepest depths of the ocean. It takes a liking to the stream of ocean bubbles, oftentimes floating amongst them.

Appearance:

It takes on the form of a hibiscus flower and a sea nettle jellyfish. When it is in a social mood, it shifts between pink and purple luminous hues. Its petals are made out of semi-transparent jelly material that flow gracefully in the water. However, when it starts to feel a little reserved, it shrinks and freezes in place, its color fading away slowly. .

Key Characteristics:

  • Glows softly
  • Has ambivert qualities
  • Extremely responsive to its surroundings

What we know so far about Jeliflora? Researchers have observed that the Jeliflora likes to wear its heart on its petals and have quickly been able to categorize its personality. It is a beautiful creature, and is open and expressive during daytime. However, it also has a shy and anxious side, often retreating into a wilted state when night approaches. Despite its jellyfish-like traits, it is not known to sting and poses no threat to humans.
Guidelines for human interaction with this creature The Jeliflora is open to human interaction, it enjoys socializing with all types of people and showing-off its beauty and majestic movements to humankind. Be mindful that when the sun goes down, the Jeliflora may not be keen to be around visitors, please give it its necessary rest and space.
