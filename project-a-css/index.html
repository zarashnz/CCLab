Scientific Name of the Creature

Jeliflora Lumina

Discovered by / At

Dr. Araz Zanhahs in the year 2028, off the coast of Pangkor Laut

Family

Belongs to Jellyfish-flower hybrid organisms

Habitat

Resides in the depths of the ocean but longs to be near the surface

Appearance

Its body resembles an orchid flower, with luminous pink and purple hues. It also has tentacles resembling that of a jellyfish. The creature's body is made of a semi-transparent jelly material that flows gracefully in the water.

Key Characteristics

When the water is dark, the Jeliflora will retract its petals inwards, wilting like a flower closing, reducing its size and reducing its glow. When the water is bright, the Jeliflora will bloom and “dance” in the water showing off its luminosity and petals.

What do we know so far about the creature?

The Jeliflora is a beautiful creature, and is open and expressive during daytime. However, it also has a shy and anxious side, often retreating into a defensive posture when dark. Despite its jellyfish-like traits, it is not known to sting and poses no threat to humans.

How do we interact with this creature?

If you touch the creature, it will squish like a jelly dessert.
